2.5 Identifying relevant sources

Saved for later

Manage and view all ‘Saved for later’ links

This section provides you with an activity which will help you refresh relevant skills in preparation for conducting your search.

Activity 11 Identifying relevant sources

Timing: Allow around 1 hour and 30 minutes for this activity.

First read the section ‘Searching databases’ in Bell, Bryman and Harley  Chapter 5 ‘Getting started: reviewing the literature’ (2019, p. 99-101).

Based on what you have learned about searching for material so far, write a short search protocol that will enable you to identify relevant literature for your own research project using the following headings:

  • keywords
  • search target (the database you will use to conduct your search)
  • parameters added (based on the options available for the database you have selected you may decide to add further parameters; for example, limiting the time span of the research, or the type of material searched)
  • date and time of initial search
  • outcomes (including the names of specific authors, books, journal articles and their reference details).

Now test your protocol and review the materials you find. Critically review the protocol and the search outcomes by answering the following questions:

  1. What was the outcome in terms of the quantity of material generated?
  2. What was the outcome in terms of the relevance of material generated?
  3. What was the outcome in terms of the quality of material generated?
  4. How would you update the protocol in order to improve these outcomes?
  5. What have you found about necessary definitions of key terms, and/or exclusions or boundaries for your research topic that will make the research useful and feasible in the time available?

Discussion

It is not possible to provide specific feedback on the individual searches that you have conducted. At this stage it is important to review and reflect on the way in which you are conducting your literature searches on a regular basis and if necessary update your overall project plan to ensure you have sufficient time allocated for this process. You might also like to refer to the section on planning your searches on the OU Library website for more information.

The remainder of your time between now and the end of Week 7 should be focused on searching the literature, and analysing the material that you have found in preparation for synthesising the review into a coherent story and writing your review. We estimate that around a third of this time will be spent on searching for and recording material and two thirds reviewing and analysing that material. In practice, the division between each activity is likely to blur as you find articles, review them and then, triggered by your findings go back and search for other areas. As you progress with your search is it likely that your search will widen and narrow, and that you will go down some pathways which prove to be dead ends. You may also find yourself seeming stuck at particular points, but do not dispair and try to move on and seek your tutor's guidance.

Managing your time carefully and making regular progress is therefore very important during this period. Your tutor will be available throughout should you need further guidance or simply to discuss particular directions you might consider taking. Using the tutor as a sounding board for your ideas can save a lot of time later. Other students in your group, may also be good critical friends during this period, particularly if their research area has some connection to yours. Swapping ideas can be a helpful way of stimulating fresh thinking about a subject.

You may also find it helpful to skim the next section of this stage, which focuses on synthesising your material and writing up your review, before you begin your search. While you will be given additional time (Weeks 8–10) to work through this, some people like to begin the process of writing up their review as they go along. Even if this is not your intention, keeping the end point of your review in mind as you embark on your search can help you if you ever suffer from writer's block.
